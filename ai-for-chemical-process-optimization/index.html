Solution / Chemical R&D

Plan smarter experiments. Reduce scale-up risk.

STOCHOS learns from the results you already have, predicts outcomes for untested conditions with quantified uncertainty, and proposes the next experiment to run. Multi-fidelity modeling links small-scale and full-scale data to cut the number of expensive runs.

Request a Demo See the experiment loop
The bottleneck

Too many variables, too few runs you can afford

Chemical R&D works across many interacting factors: reagents, concentrations, temperature, pressure, time, and equipment. Each experiment or pilot run is expensive, so teams test a fraction of the space and carry uncertainty into scale-up. STOCHOS adds a probabilistic layer that gets more out of every run.

Large parameter spaces

Many inputs interact, and full factorial testing is not realistic.

Expensive runs

Each experiment, batch, or pilot consumes time, material, and equipment.

Scale-up risk

Lab results do not always transfer cleanly to pilot and production.

Experiment planning

Choose the next run that teaches the most

Classical design of experiments fixes the plan upfront. STOCHOS updates after each result and proposes the next experiment from your objectives and constraints, adapting the search until the target is reached within fewer runs.

Process optimization

Yield, quality, and cost together

Process targets compete: higher yield can raise cost or affect quality. STOCHOS uses multi-objective Bayesian optimization to balance them and shows the trade-off rather than fixing one target at a time. Sensitivity analysis ranks which parameters drive each outcome.

How sensitivity analysis works →
Scale-up

Connect lab, pilot, and production data

Lab, pilot, and plant data differ in cost and accuracy. Multi-fidelity modeling combines them in one model: cheap, broad lab data fills the space, and sparse, expensive production data anchors accuracy, reducing the number of large-scale runs.

How multi-fidelity modeling works →
Local execution

Process recipes and conditions are sensitive IP. They stay on your own infrastructure.

STOCHOS and STOCHOS Flow are local-first. Core modeling and optimization run locally, with cloud services optional rather than required.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How does AI reduce the number of experiments?+

STOCHOS learns from your existing experiments, predicts outcomes for untested conditions with quantified uncertainty, and proposes the next run that teaches it the most. Each result updates the model, so the search adapts toward the target with fewer experiments than a fixed plan.

Can it connect lab, pilot, and production data?+

Yes. Multi-fidelity modeling combines data of different cost and accuracy in one model. Broad lab data covers the space and sparse production data anchors accuracy, which reduces the number of expensive large-scale runs needed to predict behavior at scale.

Does the software run on-premise?+

Yes. STOCHOS and STOCHOS Flow are local-first. Core modeling and optimization run on your own infrastructure, and STOCHOS Flow can use a local language model through Ollama. Optional cloud models are available if you choose them. Sensitive process data stays on your systems.
