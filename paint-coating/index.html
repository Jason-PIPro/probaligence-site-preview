Reach the target formulation with fewer lab trials.

STOCHOS learns from your formulation, measurement, and process data. It balances color, gloss, viscosity, performance, and cost together and recommends which experiment to run next, so each trial adds the information your development still needs. Sensitive recipe data stays under your control.

The bottleneck

More variables should not mean endless testing

A coating is rarely optimized for one target alone. Raw materials, concentrations, and process settings interact, and testing every combination is not realistic. STOCHOS adds an adaptive decision layer to the lab workflow: it learns from each result and chooses the next experiment that teaches it the most.

Too many combinations

Binders, additives, pigments, dispersants, concentrations, and process settings create a large search space fast.

Competing targets

Color, gloss, viscosity, durability, and cost pull against each other.

Expensive trials

Each batch and measurement costs time and material, so the order of experiments matters.

How it works

An adaptive loop, not a fixed plan

Classical design of experiments fixes one plan upfront. STOCHOS updates after every result. It starts from your trials, predicts the properties of formulations you have not made, and proposes the next experiment, adapting until the targets are met within the rules you set.

Multi-objective optimization

Balance the targets that compete

Formulation is a multi-objective problem. STOCHOS uses multi-objective Bayesian optimization to balance competing targets instead of fixing one at a time, and shows the trade-off between them. Sensitivity analysis identifies which ingredients and interactions drive each property, so you act on cause, not guesswork.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How can AI reduce lab trials in formulation development?+

STOCHOS learns from your existing trials, then recommends which formulation to test next to reach the target with fewer experiments. Each new measurement updates the model, so the search adapts. A reference example developed a coating with 17 parameters automatically within five adaptation cycles.

Can AI optimize color, gloss, viscosity, and cost together?+

Yes. Formulation is a multi-objective problem, and STOCHOS uses multi-objective Bayesian optimization to balance competing targets rather than fixing one at a time. Sensitivity analysis shows which ingredients and interactions matter most, so you can see the trade-off instead of guessing.

How does Bayesian optimization support formulation?+

It treats each lab trial as expensive and chooses the next experiment to learn the most. Starting from an initial design of experiments, the model improves with every result and proposes the next formulation, adapting until the target properties are met within the formulation rules you set.
