Solution / Paint and Coatings

Reach the target formulation with fewer lab trials.

STOCHOS learns from your coating formulation, measurement, and process data. It balances color, gloss, viscosity, performance, and cost together and recommends which experiment to run next. Each trial adds information you do not already have. Sensitive recipe data stays under your control.

The bottleneck

More variables should not mean endless testing

Raw materials, concentrations, and process settings interact, and testing every combination is not realistic.

Too many combinations

Binders, additives, pigments, dispersants, concentrations, and process settings create a large search space fast.

Competing targets

Color, gloss, viscosity, durability, and cost pull against each other.

Expensive trials

Each batch and measurement costs time and material, so the order of experiments matters.

How it works

An adaptive loop, not a fixed plan

Classical design of experiments fixes the whole plan upfront: STOCHOS updates it after every result.

Paint & Coatings · Color+

Color Match Optimization

First recipes that land close to the target on the first batch.

Open the case →

The problem

Color matching corrects one batch at a time, each batch a fresh mix, drawdown, and reading. Rush jobs stack up, and the know-how lives in a few experienced heads.

How STOCHOS fits

STOCHOS learns recipe to color from your match records and proposes the next recipe within cost limits and the pigments in stock. It flags colors it has not seen. In the demo scenario: two batches to a match, versus about seven.

Paint & Coatings · Formulation+

Reformulation Under Constraints

Rebalance every target when a raw material is pulled or regulated out.

Open the case →
all targets
rebalanced together when one raw material has to change
The problem

Change one component and every property moves: gloss, viscosity, opacity, durability, cost. A fixed design of experiments spends trials on combinations that turn out not to matter.

How STOCHOS fits

STOCHOS predicts all properties of a candidate at once, balances them with multi-objective optimization, and shows the trade-off on a Pareto front, so the recipe can land inside every target in a few cycles.

If a discontinued or regulated raw material is about to reopen a formulation, bring the family and its targets.

The problem

The decision of which formulations to carry forward needs the slowest data, weeks to a year of weathering. So teams either wait and lose months, or lock early and risk a late failure.

How STOCHOS fits

Multi-fidelity ties cheap broad measurements to a few full tests in one model. It predicts the long-duration outcome with a confidence band and sends only the uncertain candidates to the chamber.

Common questions

01How can AI reduce lab trials in formulation development?+

STOCHOS learns from your existing trials, then recommends which formulation to test next to reach the target with fewer experiments. Each new measurement updates the model, so the search adapts as results arrive. A reference example developed a coating with 17 parameters automatically within five adaptation cycles.

02Can AI optimize color, gloss, viscosity, and cost together?+

Yes. Formulation is a multi-objective problem, and STOCHOS uses multi-objective Bayesian optimization to balance competing targets rather than fixing one at a time, mapping a Pareto front of best compromises. Sensitivity analysis shows which ingredients and interactions matter most, so you can see the trade-off instead of guessing.

03How does Bayesian optimization support formulation development?+

It treats each lab trial as expensive. Starting from an initial design of experiments, the model improves with every result and proposes the next formulation with the highest expected information gain. The search adapts until the target properties are met within the formulation rules you set.

04Does our recipe data leave our infrastructure?+

Not in a local setup. STOCHOS and STOCHOS Flow are local-first: training, prediction, and optimization run on your own infrastructure, with cloud services optional rather than required. For strict environments, STOCHOS supports air-gapped deployment via an offline license, and in that mode it makes no outbound network connections. Recipes, measurements, and models stay where your IT can see them.
