Method

What is generative design?

Generative design uses AI to create geometry, not just evaluate it. STOCHOS learns the space of your existing designs, generates new and valid variants that respect real constraints, predicts how each one performs, and searches that space efficiently with Bayesian optimization through GEN-BO.

01

What generative design is

Generative design turns a handful of existing shapes into a model of the space they belong to. Instead of drawing every new variant by hand, you let the model propose new geometries that stay valid, then rank them by predicted performance. It moves the work from making one design at a time to searching many at once.

02

How STOCHOS generates and optimizes

STOCHOS learns a generative model of your geometries and pairs it with uncertainty-aware Bayesian optimization, called GEN-BO. It proposes new variants, predicts each one with quantified uncertainty, and uses that to decide which design to evaluate next, balancing exploration against exploitation. Real constraints, such as holes, mounting points, or dimensional bounds, are learned and kept intact rather than broken.

03

What it needs, in and out

You bring a small set of existing geometries and a way to score them. STOCHOS returns new, valid geometries and their predicted performance, each with a confidence attached, so you know which candidates are worth a full simulation or a prototype.

Inputs

A few existing geometries, for example STL, plus a score from a simulation, test, or KPI.

Outputs

New valid geometries and their predicted performance, each with quantified confidence.

Reference examples

From a few designs to an optimized one

In a generative-geometry reference example, three input designs were enough for STOCHOS to learn the space: training took under a minute on a GPU, new variants appeared in seconds, and boreholes were learned as constraints rather than broken. In a structural GEN-BO reference example, the search started from five designs and reached a best vertical displacement of 0.238 mm after 50 designs, with optimization under 10 minutes. Results depend on the geometry, the data, and the objective.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is this the same as generative AI that makes images?+

No. Generative design here means learning the space of engineering geometries and producing new, valid variants that respect physical constraints, then ranking them by predicted performance. It is aimed at parts and designs, not pictures or text.

How is GEN-BO different from normal optimization?+

Classical optimization searches a fixed, hand-built set of parameters. GEN-BO generates new geometries as it goes and uses uncertainty-aware Bayesian optimization to choose which to evaluate next, so it explores shapes you did not enumerate up front while spending few expensive evaluations.
