What are AI surrogate models?

A surrogate model is a fast, data-driven approximation of an expensive simulation or experiment. It learns the relationship between inputs and outputs from data you already have, then predicts results for new variants in seconds. STOCHOS builds surrogate models that also report how far to trust each prediction.

01

What a surrogate model is

Think of a surrogate as a learned shortcut. A full CFD solve or a lab experiment maps inputs to an outcome at high cost. A surrogate watches enough of those input-output pairs to approximate the same map, then answers new questions in seconds. The approximation is only as good as the data and the region it covers.

02

How STOCHOS builds surrogate models

STOCHOS uses DIM-GP, a Deep Infinite Mixture of Gaussian Processes built for small, noisy datasets, changing geometries, and multi-fidelity data. It learns the input-output relationship and returns a prediction with quantified uncertainty, so reliable interpolation is separated from risky extrapolation. It handles scalars, fields, geometries, and meshes, not just flat tables.

03

When to use one, and what goes in and out

Use a surrogate when evaluations are slow or expensive and you need many of them: design exploration, optimization loops, sensitivity studies, or real-time prediction.

Inputs

The parameters, geometries, or conditions you can change.

Outputs

The results you care about, from a single KPI to a full field, each with confidence attached.

A concrete example

A new crash case in about 20 seconds

In an LS-DYNA crash reference example, STOCHOS reproduced 32 crash models across more than 50 time steps and predicted a new case with error below 5 percent, after 21 seconds of training on a CPU. The full solver still validates the promising candidates; the surrogate makes exploring the rest affordable.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Surrogate model versus a solver?+

A solver computes the physics from first principles at high cost. A surrogate model approximates the solver's input-output behavior from existing data and predicts new cases in seconds. The surrogate does not replace the solver; it reduces how often you need to run it, and the solver still validates key results.

How much data does a surrogate model need?+

It depends on the problem, but STOCHOS is built for small, expensive datasets rather than large ones. The DIM-GP approach is designed for tens of samples, noisy measurements, and multi-fidelity data, and it reports where confidence is low so you can add data where it helps most.
